Maura Lane

I am a junior at Millersville University of Pennsylvania majoring in Interactive and Graphic Design. I have yet to find a type of design I dislike, but I particularly enjoy logo design and working in Adobe AfterEffects for motion design.

Patterns and puns are my greatest inspirations for design. I like working abstractly and seeing where my mind takes me, and making patterns in Adobe Illustrator was the way I did that while familiarizing myself with the software.

Finding multiple meanings to a word or prompt is a great starting point that can create the fun visual elements for a design. Professional work may not always call for fun, but approaching a mundane project with humor can lead to creative decisions.

The works below are a mix of class projects and personal projects.

Logos

Class prompt: Design a logo based on an animal

I chose an electric eel and used a lightening bolt as the motif. Magneel makes shockingly good microwaves.
Class prompt: Design a logo based on a fruit

I chose a key lime as my fruit and I decided a key smith would be fitting. The shape of a lime mirrors the shape of the top a key, so I worked with the silhouette of the lime to make a key for the logo
Class prompt: Design a logo based on a plant

I chose a lily pad as my plant. After doing research on the plant and what its shape reminds me of, I settled on an umbrella company. Velari means a type of awning or covering.
Class prompt: Design a logo based on a vegetable.

I chose cellery as my vegetable. My ideas stemmed from the homophone of 'cell' and 'sell'. The stringy texture of the cellery represents a bar graph. Cellers would be a stock market consultanting business.
I made this logo after hearing 'subtlety' as 'subtle tea'. I like designs that are minimalistic and don't have overwhelming branding. The light-weight typeface and tea leaf on the edge of the y are subtle while showing that the company sells green tea.
Class prompt: Design a logo based on a mineral

I chose aquamarine as my mineral. I had the loosest approach with this logo as the mineral itself did not give me many ideas, but seperating ‘aqua’ and ‘marine’ sparked the idea of a submarine company. The Saturn logo, bubble with a ring, inspired the name to be Aquarn. The company would make submarines that look like spaceships.

Poem Book

This poem book was made spring semester of my sophmore year in an illustration course.

I wrote this short poem in a an introduction to web programming course my freshman year. I revisited it after hearing the prompt to illustrate a story, lyric, or poem. The illustration was done in Illustrator. I experimented with texture to elevate the flat graphics.

Explainer Video

This video was made spring semester of my sophmore year in a motion design course.

I worked on this with a partner to explain how honey bees make honey. We decided on a graphic style and used storyboards for planning before animating it in Aftereffects.
